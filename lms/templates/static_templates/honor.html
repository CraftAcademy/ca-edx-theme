The education

The regular Course consists of a twelve weeks fast-paced and intensive programming “bootcamp”, where you as a Student are required to take active part in the education in order to maximize your personal outcome and put serious effort into learning to program and to develop your programming skills.

You should be prepared that the Course generally requires more time than 8 hours of studies a day and that for the best outcome you will have to devote yourself to the education during the Course period. The Education requires that you are able to work individually as well as in teams.

Course content and structure

No previous programming skills are necessary in order to partake in a Course, although a basic understanding of programming and basic computer skills are needed. Craft Academy may therefore, at its discretion, require you to complete a pre-course prior to the start of the regular Course (see further information on this below under the section “Pre-course”). Each regular Course lasts for 12 calendar weeks (including Swedish bank holidays) and education is provided in class at site Mondays to Fridays (except for during bank holidays). Students may after agreement with Craft Academy partake in a Course partly from distance. However, it is strongly recommended that you partake physically for best results and some of the Course elements require physical presence.

Courses are held in English or when appropriate in Swedish (only when all Students are Swedish speaking). The course contains elements of teacher-led education, individual and team-based workshops, self-tuition, problem solving, writing of software etc. The Course is ended with a workshop where the Students are required to create a functioning application or other software that could be used in the marketplace (the “Final Project”). However, the content, structure and methods used in each Course are subject to a constant process of review and improvement. This means that the Course that you attend may not be identical to previous Courses, and whilst it will be materially the same, it may not be identical to that described on the Craft Academy website or otherwise in Craft Academy marketing materials. The curriculum of the course is presented to you not later than on the commencement of the Course. As the course continuously evolves, the curriculum applied in the Course may also change from time to time and deviate from what has been presented to you earlier on the website or in other marketing materials.

Course materials and literature will be provided during the Course. You will need a portable computer that meets the minimum requirements of Craft Academy in order to partake in the Course. If you are uncertain of whether your computer meets the requirements, please contact Craft Academy prior to registering. It is your obligation to ensure that your computer meets the requirements and can be used in the Course.

Deviations from the above general content and structure may apply for certain special Courses provided by Craft Academy. Structure and content for such Courses is provided separately to the Students. These Terms apply in all applicable parts also to such Courses.
